Miles Davis

jazz composer and trumpeter

Here's a time line of Miles Davis Life:

  • NOV 18, 1926 - Born in East St. Louis to a middle class family. Played in many bands and always carried his trumpet around with him. His child years were spent playing sports with his freinds and devoting himself to music.
  • SEP 16, 1944 - Went to Julliard to study music and hated because it was extremely biast towards white people and they didn't teach jazz.
  • JAN 10, 1945 - Miles was doing gigs and recording records with charlie Parker and Dizzy Gillespie the two jazz masters of the time.
  • JAN 18, 1950 - from 1950 to 1954 he went through a heroin addiction effecting his playing.
  • MAY 19, 1957 - He put together a group of jazz musicians and brass instruments that were not comon in jazz to make a bepob orchestral sound that changed the sounds of modern jazz.
  • MAY 21, 1960 - He started to play with electronic instruments getting him even more fans. He played with John Mclaughlin, Chick Corea, Herbie Hancock, Jimi Hendrix and many other great artists.
  • APR 24, 1972 - Miles got in a car accident and took a break for five years
  • JUL 23, 1982 - He won 8 different grammy awards for his music. And several other awards.
  • MAY 21, 1991 - He made his most memorable appearence when he played with the Quincy Troupe orchestra at the Montreux Jazz Festival. Then he died three months later from a stroke bringing sadness to many thousands of fans.
